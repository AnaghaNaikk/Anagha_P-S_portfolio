Chapter 2: Probability Fundamentals

Meaning: Probability measures how likely events are to happen (0 = impossible, 1 = certain). This chapter studies basic rules for calculating probabilities, how events relate, and how new information updates our beliefs about likelihoods.

  • Predict future events

Chapter 3: Probability Distributions

Meaning: Distributions describe patterns of how data values are spread. Different types model different situations - coin flips (binomial), rare events (Poisson), or normal bell-shaped patterns that appear everywhere in nature.

  • Understand data patterns
  • Predict ranges of values
  • Most data follows normal distribution

Chapter 4: Sampling & Estimation

Meaning: We can't study entire populations, so we take samples and make estimates about the whole. This chapter explains how to select good samples and calculate confidence intervals showing how accurate our estimates are.

  • Study populations through samples
  • Calculate confidence in results
  • Real-world decision making
